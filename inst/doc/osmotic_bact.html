Hyperosmotic Stress in Bacteria

This vignette from the R package JMDplots version 1.2.19-4 shows chemical metrics for proteins that are differentially expressed in hyperosmotic compared to control conditions. The analysis is described in more detail in a paper (Dick et al., 2020). Abbreviations:

  • ZC – average oxidation state of carbon.
  • nH2O – stoichiometric hydration state per residue (Dick et al., 2020) computed using the QEC basis species (glutamine, glutamic acid, cysteine, H2O, O2).
  • pI – isoelectric point; GRAVY – grand average of hydropathicity.
  • nAA – protein length; MW – molecular weight per residue.
datasets <- pdat_osmotic_bact(2020)

Differences are calculated as (median value for up-regulated proteins) - (median value for down-regulated proteins). Dashed lines enclose the 50% confidence region for highest probability density.

In the table, values of ΔZC, ΔnH2O, and ΔGRAVY are multiplied by 1000, values of ΔpI and ΔMW are multiplied by 100, and negative values are shown in bold. Abbreviations:

  • ndown – number of down-regulated proteins; nup – number of up-regulated proteins; aw – water activity; LB – lysogeny broth; WT – wild-type; MMO – medium minus osmoprotectants.
