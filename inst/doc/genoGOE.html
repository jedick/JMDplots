Genomes record the Great Oxidation Event (2024)

This vignette runs the functions to make the plots from the following manuscript:

Dick JM. 2024. Genomes record the Great Oxidation Event. Manuscript in preparation.

This vignette was compiled on 2024-07-03 with JMDplots 1.2.20 and CHNOSZ 2.1.0.

library(JMDplots)

Protein oxidation is pervasive in genomes of methanogens that diverged after the GOE (Figure 1)

genoGOE_1()

Data sources: List of Class I and II methanogen species (Lyu and Lu, 2018), archaeal marker genes used in GTDB (Rinke et al., 2021), and protein abundance in Methanococcus maripaludis (Xia et al., 2006).

Gene age groups in eukaryotic genomes show protein oxidation around the GOE and NOE (Figure 2)

genoGOE_2()

Data sources: Consensus gene ages (Liebeskind et al., 2016), protein sequences (The UniProt Consortium, 2023), and divergence times (Kumar et al., 2022).

Oxidation of reconstructed ancestral Rubisco sequences around the GOE corresponds to high-Eh conditions (Figure 3)

genoGOE_3()

Data source: Reconstructed ancestral sequences of Rubisco (Kacar et al., 2017).
