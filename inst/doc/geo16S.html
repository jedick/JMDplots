Chemical metrics of estimated microbial community proteomes from 16S rRNA and RefSeq data (2021)

This vignette runs the code to make the plots from the following paper:

Dick JM, Tan J. 2021. Chemical links between redox conditions and microbial community composition deduced from 16S rRNA and reference protein sequences. Submitted manuscript.

Preprint available at:

Dick JM, Tan J. 2021. Genomic evidence for a chemical link between redox conditions and microbial community composition. bioRxiv. doi: 10.1101/2021.05.31.446500

This vignette was compiled on 2021-12-31 with JMDplots 1.2.10.

library(JMDplots)
# Tell the functions where the RDP Classifer output is stored (this is the default)
geo16Sdir = system.file("extdata/geo16S", package = "JMDplots")
options(chem16Sdir = geo16Sdir)

Distinct chemical parameters of reference proteomes for major taxonomic groups (Figure 1)

dat1 <- geo16S1()

Data source: NCBI Reference Sequence (RefSeq) database (OLeary et al., 2016). Numbered symbols: (1) Methanococci, (2) Archaeoglobi, (3) Thermococci, (4) Halobacteria, (5) Nanohaloarchaea, (6) Clostridia.

Get the chemical metrics including ZC for particular genera that are abundant in produced fluids of shale gas wells:

datadir <- system.file("extdata/chem16S", package = "JMDplots")
taxon_metrics <- read.csv(file.path(datadir, "taxon_metrics.csv"), as.is = TRUE)
subset(taxon_metrics, group %in% c("Halanaerobium", "Thermoanaerobacter"))

Estimated community proteomes from different environments have distinct chemical signatures (Figure 2)

dat2 <- geo16S2()

Data sources: Guerrero Negro mat (Harris et al., 2013), Yellowstone hot springs (Bowen De Len et al., 2013), Baltic Sea water (Herlemann et al., 2016), Lake Fryxell mat (Jungblut et al., 2016), Tibetan Plateau lakes (Zhong et al., 2016), Manus Basin vents (Meier et al., 2017), Qarhan Salt Lake soils (Xie et al., 2017), Black Sea water (Sollai et al., 2019).

Lower carbon oxidation state is tied to oxygen depletion in water columns (Figure 3)

dat3 <- geo16S3()

Data sources: Black Sea (Sollai et al., 2019), Swiss lakes (Lake Zug and Lake Lugano) (Mayr et al., 2020), Eastern Tropical North Pacific (ETNP) (Ganesh et al., 2015), Sansha Yongle Blue Hole (He et al., 2020), Ursu Lake (Baricz et al., 2021).

Changes of abundance and chemical metrics at different taxonomic levels (Figure 4)

geo16S4()

Data sources: Manus Basin vents (Meier et al., 2017), Baltic Sea water (Herlemann et al., 2016).

Decreased carbon oxidation state of estimated proteomes for communities affected by shale gas extraction (Figure 5)

dat4 <- geo16S5()

Data sources: Northwestern Pennsylvania stream water and sediment (Ulrich et al., 2018), Pennsylvania State Forests stream water in spring and fall (Mumford et al., 2020), Marcellus Shale (Cluff et al., 2014), DenverJulesburg Basin (Hull et al., 2018), Duvernay Formation (Zhong et al., 2019).
