Thermodynamic model for water activity and redox potential in evolution and development (2022)

This vignette runs the code to make the plots from the following paper:

Dick JM. 2022. A thermodynamic model for water activity and redox potential in evolution and development. Submitted manuscript.

Preprint available at:

Dick JM. 2021. Redox potential linked to water loss from proteins in evolution and development. bioRxiv. doi: 10.1101/2021.01.29.428804

This vignette was compiled on 2021-12-31 with JMDplots 1.2.10, CHNOSZ 1.4.1, and canprot 1.1.1.

library(JMDplots)

To reduce running time, the plots in this vignette are made with 99 bootstrap replicates. To reproduce the plots in the paper, the boot.R argument should be changed to 999.

Protein length and chemical metrics for phylostratigraphic age groups (Figure 1)

evdevH2O1(boot.R = 99)

Data sources: Phylostrata are from Trigos et al. (2017). Consensus gene ages are from Liebeskind et al. (2016).

MaximAct: Thermodynamic analysis of optimal logaH2O and logfO2 for target proteins (Figure 2)

evdevH2O2()

Optimal logaH2O and logfO2 and virtual Eh for target proteins (Figure 3)

evdevH2O3()

Data sources: Plasma and subcellular redox potentials are from van t Erve et al. (2013) and Jones and Sies (2015).

Chemical metrics for and thermodynamic parameters with different background proteomes (Figure 4)

evdevH2O4()

Chemical and thermodynamic analysis of B. subtilis biofilm transcriptome and proteome (Figure 5)

evdevH2O5(boot.R = 99)

Data source: Transcriptomic and proteomic data are from Futo et al. (2021).

Organismal water content, proteomic nH2O, and optimal logaH2O for fruit fly development (Figure 6)

evdevH2O6(boot.R = 99)

Data sources: a Whole-organism water content is from Church and Robertson (1966). b Stoichiometric hydration state of proteins is calculated in this study using proteomic data from Casas-Vila et al. (2017). d ZC and nH2O of differentially expressed proteins in embryos and adult flies is calculated in this study using proteomic data from Fabre et al. (2019).

Evolution of protein ZC in eukaryotic lineages (Figure 7)

evdevH2O7()

Data sources: a Consensus gene ages are from Liebeskind et al. (2016). Divergence times of human lineage are from Kumar et al. (2017). b Amino acid compositions of homology groups for Pfam domains are from James et al. (2020) and James et al. (2021).
