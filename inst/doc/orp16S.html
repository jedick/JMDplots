Chemical adaptation of bacterial communities to redox potential (2022)

This vignette runs the code to make the plots from the following paper:

Dick JM, Meng D. 2022. Estimated proteomes of bacterial communities are chemically adapted to redox conditions at a global scale. Submitted manuscript.

Preprint available at bioRxiv. doi: 10.1101/2021.10.12.464155

This vignette was compiled on 2022-09-23 with JMDplots 1.2.14.

library(JMDplots)

Outline of methods (Figure 1)

orp16S1()

Chemical depth profiles in Winogradsky columns (Figure 2)

orp16S2()

Data sources: ORP and pH for Eh7 calculation (Diez-Ercilla et al., 2019); 16S rRNA gene sequences for ZC calculation (Rundell et al., 2014).

Sample locations on world map (Figure 3)

orp16S3()

Data sources: coastlineWorld dataset in R package oce (Kelley and Richards, 2021); shapefiles for North American Great Lakes (USGS, 2010).

Linear regressions between Eh7 and ZC at local scales (Figure 4)

orp16S4()

Data sources (a): Daya Bay (Wu et al., 2021), Bay of Biscay (Lanzén et al., 2021), Hunan Province (Meng et al., 2019).

Data sources (c): 1 acidic and 2 circumneutral to alkaline New Zealand hot springs (Power et al., 2018), 3 Dallol Geothermal Area (Belilla et al., 2019), 4 Eastern Tibetan Plateau (Guo et al., 2020), 5 Uzon Caldera (Peltek et al., 2020), 6 Southern Tibetan Plateau (Ma et al., 2021).
